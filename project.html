PROJECT: Nova

PROJECT: Nova is 2D top down boss rush game set in a dystopian cyberpunk world where the player (Nova) has to reach the top floor to save her sister, captured by an evil organisation. The game includes puzzle mechanics as well as shooting down enemies. The game is topped off by a boss level at the end. The game has been influenced by different sources such as movies and other video games. It was heavily inspired by futuristic movies such as Ultra Violet, Ghost in The Shell and more. Gameplay mechanics were examples from Enter the Gungeon, Transistor and Invisible Inc. Art style was a merge between pixel and semi-realistic art but it was mainly influenced by the GameBoy Pokemon games.

PROJECT: Nova isn’t just about fighting enemies and solving puzzles but also includes narrative elements to add depth to the story which some retro, shooter games lack as they are only focusing on the action. The objective was to connect the players with the characters in the game. This game was made for the Final Major Project for Year 12 with a team of four. I was the games director and I had three other team members who were the programmer, artist and sound engineer. I have mostly done the programming elements of this game including the player controller, shooting mechanic and creating the UI and HUD in C#.

  • Year 1 final project
  • Group project
  • Time: 15 weeks
  • Made with Unity; art made using Photoshop and Aseprite
  • Grade achieved: Distiction

    The Lazarus Murder

    The Lazarus Murder is a 3D first person horror game where the player explores a house to find out clues about a murder that happened a few years ago. The player plays as a journalist who has been requested to explore the crime scene of a closed down case about a murder of an orphan girl. Due to the police not finding any clues, the case was dismissed. But it is opened again and the player finds that there is more to the murder; something supernatural.

  • The game was an independent project – the game is not completely finished yet but has an end game after the player has reached the last room in the house.
  • Made with Unreal Engine and visual blueprinting.
  • 3D modelling done by using Maya and texturing done with Mudbox and Photoshop.
  • The game was intended to be played with VR but it mainly works well on the computer.
  • Grade achieved: Merit

    • Cooper's Course

    Cooper’s Course is a game jam project with a team of four. I played the part of the programmer. The game jam was judged by people from Creative Assembly, Machinima XBOC and Sports Interactive. This was my first game jam and it was very good experience. The game is a platformer featuring a dog who is running through a winter forest to get to its owner.

  • The game has a unique art style of watercolour textures.
  • It was created in Unity, C#.
  • I did all the programming for the game and one of the interesting mechanic is that the dog has to move crates to jump to the next platform. The game is not completed and has no proper end game but it is playable!
